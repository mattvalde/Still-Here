Resources

Explore museums, historical archives, cultural centers, and educational organizations connected to Jewish history, Holocaust memory, and Polish Jewish life.

POLIN Museum

The POLIN Museum of the History of Polish Jews is a museum in Poland, located in the former Warsaw ghetto, dedicated to preserving the 1,000-year history of Jewish life in Poland through interactive exhibits, education, and cultural dialogue. The museum aims to promote historical understanding, remembrance, and mutual respect while confronting antisemitism and discrimination.

1,000 Years of History of Polish Jews

The core exhibition is a journey through the 1,000-year history of Polish Jews. Discover how Jews first arrived in Polish lands, why they stayed, and how Poland became home to one of the largest Jewish communities in the world.

Auschwitz Museum

Auschwitz-Birkenau was the largest Nazi concentration and extermination camp complex during World War II, where over one million people, mostly Jews, were murdered during the Holocaust. Today, the Auschwitz-Birkenau Memorial and Museum preserves the camp to educate visitors about the horrors of the Holocaust and honor the memory of its victims.

JCC of Krakow

The JCC is a modern-day Jewish Community Center in Krakow. It helps individuals impacted by the Holocaust through community service, supplies such as food, and provides a place for Jewish culture and life. It also currently helps Ukrainian refugees impacted by the Russo-Ukrainian War.

Schindler’s Factory

Oskar Schindler was a Nazi official who helped over a thousand Jews escape death. His factory is now a modern exhibit that teaches visitors about Krakow during World War II and remembers his actions and legacy.

Forum for Dialogue

The Forum for Dialogue works to preserve sites of Jewish significance in Poland so that their history within the nation is not erased. They also help show people in Poland the rich Jewish history that has existed there and create modern-day connections between Jewish people and Polish people.

Majdanek Museum

Majdanek was a Nazi concentration and extermination camp located near Lublin, Poland, that operated from 1941 to 1944 during World War II. Today, the campgrounds, barracks, gas chambers, and memorials are preserved to educate visitors about the Holocaust and honor the victims who suffered and died there.
